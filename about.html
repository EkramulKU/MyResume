About Me

Ekramul Haque is an environmental researcher and journalist based in Khulna, Bangladesh. He is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Forestry at Khulna University, where his research focuses on land use and land cover dynamics, environmental change, and conservation policy analysis. His academic work integrates remote sensing and GIS techniques, particularly using Google Earth Engine and ArcGIS Pro, to assess ecological transformations in sensitive ecosystems such as the Sundarbans.

Alongside his academic pursuits, Ekramul actively contributes to journalism, working with several national newspapers in Bangladesh. His reporting primarily covers environmental issues, climate change, forest conservation, and socio-ecological challenges. Through his writing, he aims to bridge the gap between scientific research and public awareness, translating complex environmental data into accessible narratives.

Ekramul has gained hands-on field experience through research collaborations, including work with Friendship NGO, where he contributed to mangrove ecosystem studies in the coastal region of Bangladesh. His fieldwork includes data collection, community engagement, and environmental monitoring, strengthening his understanding of real-world ecological challenges.

He has also played significant leadership roles in student organizations. As the President of the Khulna University Journalists’ Association (KUJA), he organized national-level journalism training programs, led media coordination efforts, and managed editorial and publication activities. His leadership extended to fostering collaboration among campus journalists and promoting ethical media practices.

His technical expertise includes GIS and remote sensing, data analysis, and environmental research methods. He is proficient in tools such as ArcGIS Pro, Google Earth Engine, and Microsoft Office, along with programming knowledge in Python and C++. His interdisciplinary skill set enables him to combine scientific analysis with effective communication.

Ekramul completed his Bachelor of Science in Forestry from Khulna University and is expected to complete his Master's degree in 2026. His long-term research interests include environmental monitoring, forest and climate interactions, and sustainable resource management. He is particularly interested in using geospatial technologies to support evidence-based environmental decision-making.

Through a combination of research, journalism, and leadership, Ekramul Haque continues to build a multidisciplinary career focused on environmental sustainability, scientific communication, and public engagement.
