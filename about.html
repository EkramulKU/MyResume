About Me

Ekramul Haque is an environmental researcher and journalist based in Khulna, Bangladesh. He is currently pursuing a Master of Science in Forestry at Khulna University, focusing on land use and land cover dynamics, environmental monitoring, and conservation policy. His research integrates advanced geospatial technologies including Google Earth Engine and ArcGIS Pro to analyze ecological changes, particularly within the Sundarbans Ecologically Critical Area.

Alongside his academic work, he is actively engaged in journalism, contributing to multiple national newspapers. His reporting focuses on environmental issues, climate change, forestry, and socio-ecological challenges. Through his writing, he aims to make scientific knowledge accessible to the public and support evidence-based decision-making.

He is currently preparing for competitive government positions, fact-checking roles, and first-class officer positions in universities, aiming to build a career that combines research, policy, and communication.
